One Hour

Just one hour a month is all it takes to foster connectivity with an Old Mate. Need some help with what to do for that hour? We have you covered:
(Each month is presented as a case study of an Old Mate and their hobbies. Each month expands when clicked on to show further detail)

Month 1: Chess

This is Roy. Roy lost his wife a few years ago and has been fairly isolated since. His family mostly live interstate, and those who live close are always very busy. Roy used to play Chess with his wife and really misses the games they had together. Roy has said he would love for someone to come and play chess with him sometimes; as it’s one of his favourite pastimes.

Month 2: Gardening

Bob is Jill’s grandfather and he loves gardening. As Bob has gotten older, Bob isn’t as mobile as he used to be. Jill goes around to Bob’s house for an hour a month and helps him with some of the trickier yard work that he can no longer do safely. Bob loves the time he gets to spend with Jill and he also loves seeing his garden flourish! #oldmate

Month 3: Volunteering at an aged-care facility

This is Geoff. Geoff’s family are overseas and the social interaction he gets comes only from activities organised by the aged car facility where he lives. Geoff would never ask for help or support; it’s not how his generation was raised to operate, but Geoff gets very lonely with no-one to interact with. Take a pack of cards, your ipod or a chessboard and visit Geoff or someone like him in your local aged care facility. #oldmate
It’s not just the Old Mates in your life that need someone, there are a lot of men in Australia who are in need of friends and support networks. Volunteering at an aged-care facility is a great way to connect with Old Mates who really need it:
